Fiber Bundle Endomicrscopy

Fiber bundle endomicroscopy is a cutting-edge medical imaging technology that uses a flexible bundle of optical fibers to transmit high-resolution images taken inside the body to the outside of the body. This technique allows real-time visualization of internal structures and is valuable in a number of medical fields, including gastroenterology and pulmonology.

Endoscopes can be used to look at a number of parts of the body, such as the gastrointestinal tract, Barrett's esophagus, colorectal cancer, gastric cancer, urinary tract, and lungs, among many others [1].
